# Volume 07 — Community, Social Systems & Moderation Guidelines

## Project: UNO Reimagined (Working Title)

**Version:** 1.0
**Document Type:** Community, Social Systems & Moderation Guidelines (CSMG)
**Status:** Approved (Draft v1.0)
**Audience:** Product Managers, Community Managers, Game Designers, UX Designers, Moderators, QA Engineers, Customer Support

---

# 1. Document Purpose

This document defines how players interact with each other beyond gameplay.

UNO is fundamentally a social game.

Winning matches creates excitement.

Playing with friends creates memories.

A healthy community increases retention more than almost any feature.

The objective of this document is to define how players communicate, build friendships, compete respectfully, and enjoy a safe online environment.

---

# 2. Community Vision

We are not building a card game.

We are building a place where people enjoy spending time together.

Players should feel:

* Welcome
* Safe
* Respected
* Included
* Connected

The community should naturally encourage players to invite friends and return regularly.

---

# 3. Community Principles

Every community feature must follow these principles.

### Respect

Treat every player with dignity.

---

### Fairness

Every player receives equal treatment regardless of skill level.

---

### Inclusion

Everyone should feel comfortable participating.

---

### Safety

Players should be protected from harassment, abuse, and inappropriate behavior.

---

### Positive Competition

Winning should never require disrespecting opponents.

---

# 4. Social Philosophy

The best multiplayer experiences are created through shared moments.

The platform should encourage:

* Playing together.
* Celebrating together.
* Improving together.
* Returning together.

Social systems should reduce loneliness rather than increase competition.

---

# 5. Friends System

Players should be able to:

* Add Friends
* Accept Requests
* Decline Requests
* Remove Friends
* Block Players
* Favorite Friends

Friends should remain accessible from every major screen.

---

# 6. Friend Requests

Friend requests should be simple.

Possible sources:

* Recent Match
* Search Username
* Invite Link
* QR Code
* Contact Import (Optional)

Adding a friend should require minimal effort.

---

# 7. Friend Status

Friends may display:

* Online
* Playing
* In Lobby
* In Match
* Away
* Offline

Privacy settings should allow players to hide their status.

---

# 8. Invite System

Inviting friends should never require more than two clicks.

Supported methods:

* Room Code
* Direct Invite
* Share Link
* QR Code

Joining a friend should feel effortless.

---

# 9. Recent Players

Maintain a list of recently played opponents.

Players may:

* Add Friend
* Invite Again
* View Profile
* Report
* Block

Recent interactions often become future friendships.

---

# 10. Private Rooms

Private rooms should encourage group play.

Hosts control:

* Room visibility
* Password
* House Rules
* AI Players
* Spectators
* Match Start

Private rooms are intended for families, friends, schools, and communities.

---

# 11. Clubs (Future Expansion)

Players may create Clubs.

A Club represents a long-term community.

Possible features:

* Club Name
* Club Logo
* Club Leaderboard
* Club Chat
* Club Events
* Club Achievements

Clubs strengthen long-term retention.

---

# 12. Chat Philosophy

Chat should improve gameplay, not distract from it.

Chat must remain:

* Simple
* Friendly
* Optional

Players should never feel forced to engage.

---

# 13. Chat Types

Supported communication:

* Lobby Chat
* Private Chat
* Friend Chat
* Club Chat
* Party Chat

Ranked gameplay chat may be limited to reduce toxicity.

---

# 14. Quick Reactions

Quick reactions provide expressive communication without disrupting gameplay.

Examples:

👍 Nice Move

👏 Well Played

😂 Lucky!

😮 Wow!

🎉 GG

❤️ Thanks

Quick reactions reduce language barriers.

---

# 15. Emotes

Emotes should remain:

* Friendly
* Positive
* Family-friendly

Avoid sarcastic or insulting reactions.

Emotes should celebrate the game rather than mock opponents.

---

# 16. Voice Communication

Voice chat is optional.

Recommendations:

* Friends only
* Party only
* Disabled by default for public matchmaking

Players should retain complete control over voice participation.

---

# 17. Community Events

Regular community events encourage participation.

Examples:

* Weekend Challenge
* Global Card Event
* Community Goals
* Holiday Tournament
* Anniversary Celebration

Events should encourage collaboration rather than exclusion.

---

# 18. Community Recognition

Recognize positive contributions.

Possible recognition:

* Helpful Player Badge
* Community Champion
* Friendly Opponent
* Event Contributor
* Veteran Player

Recognition encourages positive behavior.

---

# 19. Code of Conduct

Every player agrees to:

* Respect others.
* Play fairly.
* Avoid harassment.
* Avoid discrimination.
* Avoid cheating.
* Avoid offensive usernames.
* Report inappropriate behavior responsibly.

The Code of Conduct applies everywhere within the platform.

---

# 20. Acceptable Behavior

Examples:

* Congratulating opponents.
* Helping new players.
* Playing honestly.
* Completing matches.
* Respecting differing skill levels.

Positive behavior should be celebrated.

---

# 21. Unacceptable Behavior

Examples include:

* Harassment
* Hate speech
* Threats
* Spam
* Offensive usernames
* Match sabotage
* Intentional inactivity
* Exploiting bugs
* Account sharing
* Boosting

These behaviors damage the community experience.

---

# 22. Username Policy

Usernames must not include:

* Hate speech
* Profanity
* Personal information
* Impersonation
* Sexual content
* Illegal content
* Offensive language

Usernames should remain appropriate for players of all ages.

---

# 23. Avatar Guidelines

Profile images should avoid:

* Explicit content
* Violence
* Hate symbols
* Illegal activity
* Personal identification without consent

Community safety extends to visual identity.

---

# 24. Reporting System

Players may report:

* Offensive Username
* Harassment
* Cheating
* AFK Behavior
* Match Manipulation
* Inappropriate Chat
* Bug Exploitation

Reporting should require only a few simple steps.

---

# 25. Report Philosophy

Reports should be:

* Easy to submit.
* Reviewed fairly.
* Protected against abuse.

False reporting should not become a tool for harassment.

---

# 26. Moderation Principles

Moderators should remain:

* Fair
* Consistent
* Transparent
* Respectful
* Evidence-based

Every moderation decision should be explainable.

---

# 27. Penalty Ladder

Progressive enforcement encourages learning.

Recommended sequence:

First Offense

Educational Warning

↓

Second Offense

Temporary Restriction

↓

Third Offense

Longer Suspension

↓

Repeated Severe Violations

Permanent Ban

Not every violation requires maximum punishment immediately.

---

# 28. Immediate Permanent Ban

Certain behaviors justify immediate removal.

Examples:

* Hate speech
* Threats of violence
* Child exploitation
* Illegal activity
* Severe cheating
* Impersonating staff

Player safety takes priority.

---

# 29. Blocking Players

Blocked players cannot:

* Send messages.
* Send friend requests.
* Invite to rooms.
* View limited profile information.

Blocking should immediately improve the player's experience.

---

# 30. Privacy

Players control:

* Online status.
* Friend requests.
* Match history visibility.
* Profile visibility.
* Spectator permissions.

Privacy settings should be easy to understand.

---

# 31. Spectator Etiquette

Spectators should never influence gameplay.

Spectators may:

* Watch
* React
* Cheer

Spectators may not:

* Reveal hidden information.
* Distract active players.
* Abuse chat.

---

# 32. Community Health Metrics

Monitor:

* Reports per Match
* AFK Rate
* Chat Abuse Rate
* Match Completion
* Friend Requests
* Rematch Rate
* Club Participation

Healthy communities create healthier products.

---

# 33. New Player Protection

New players deserve a welcoming experience.

Recommendations:

* Beginner matchmaking.
* Friendly onboarding.
* Helpful tips.
* Limited exposure to toxic behavior.

First impressions strongly influence retention.

---

# 34. Returning Player Experience

Returning players should receive:

* Welcome Back message.
* Summary of new content.
* Friend activity.
* Current events.
* Easy path into a match.

Returning should feel familiar.

---

# 35. Community Events Calendar

Suggested recurring schedule:

Daily

* Daily Missions

Weekly

* Weekend Challenge

Monthly

* Community Tournament

Quarterly

* Ranked Season

Yearly

* Anniversary Festival

Predictable events encourage long-term participation.

---

# 36. Sportsmanship

Good sportsmanship includes:

* Saying "Good Game."
* Completing matches.
* Respecting opponents.
* Helping beginners.
* Celebrating fairly.

Sportsmanship should be recognized throughout the product.

---

# 37. Community Recognition Program

Reward players who improve the community.

Examples:

* Helpful Mentor
* Fair Competitor
* Positive Player
* Event Organizer
* Club Leader

Recognition is more effective than punishment alone.

---

# 38. Community Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

* Toxic leaderboards.
* Public shaming.
* Excessive punishment.
* Hidden moderation.
* Anonymous harassment.
* Forced social interaction.

Players should always feel in control of their experience.

---

# 39. Community Design Checklist

Before launching a new community feature, verify:

* Does it encourage positive interaction?
* Does it protect player safety?
* Is moderation straightforward?
* Does it respect privacy?
* Does it strengthen friendships?
* Does it reduce toxicity?
* Does it align with the product vision?

Only features that satisfy these principles should be approved.

---

# 40. Summary

This Community, Social Systems & Moderation Guidelines document establishes the foundation for a respectful, welcoming, and engaging multiplayer environment. By emphasizing positive social interaction, clear behavioral expectations, fair moderation, and player safety, the platform aims to foster lasting relationships and a vibrant community.

Community features should always enhance the core gameplay experience rather than distract from it. When players feel safe, respected, and connected, they are more likely to return, invite friends, and contribute positively to the long-term success of the game.
