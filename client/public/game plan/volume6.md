# Volume 06 — Competitive Design & Ranking System

## Project: UNO Reimagined (Working Title)

**Version:** 1.0
**Document Type:** Competitive Design & Ranking System (CDRS)
**Status:** Approved (Draft v1.0)
**Audience:** Product Managers, Game Designers, UX Designers, Community Managers, Tournament Organizers, QA Engineers

---

# 1. Document Purpose

This document defines the competitive ecosystem of UNO Reimagined.

The objective is to create a ranked environment where:

* Skill is rewarded.
* Matchmaking is fair.
* Progress feels meaningful.
* Competition remains healthy.
* Every season offers a fresh challenge.

Competitive play should never feel unfair, manipulated, or inaccessible.

---

# 2. Competitive Vision

Ranked mode exists for players who want to improve, measure their skill, and compete against others of similar ability.

The experience should emphasize:

* Fairness
* Transparency
* Consistency
* Long-term progression
* Prestige
* Sportsmanship

Winning should feel earned, and losses should provide opportunities to learn rather than discourage continued play.

---

# 3. Competitive Design Principles

Every competitive feature must satisfy these principles.

### Fair Competition

Every player begins under the same conditions.

---

### Skill Over Luck

Although UNO includes randomness, the ranked experience should reward decision-making, probability assessment, risk management, and consistency over many matches.

---

### Respect Player Time

Competitive sessions should be meaningful without requiring excessive play.

---

### Transparent Progression

Players should always understand:

* Why they gained rank.
* Why they lost rank.
* What is required to advance.

---

### Healthy Competition

The system should encourage improvement rather than frustration.

---

# 4. Ranked Eligibility

Players must satisfy basic requirements before entering ranked mode.

Recommended requirements:

* Complete tutorial.
* Finish 10 casual matches.
* Reach Account Level 10.
* Maintain a verified account (optional).
* No active disciplinary restrictions.

These requirements reduce smurfing and help ensure players understand the game before competing.

---

# 5. Ranked Match Format

Standard competitive matches use:

| Setting        | Value      |
| -------------- | ---------- |
| Players        | 4          |
| Official Rules | Enabled    |
| House Rules    | Disabled   |
| Turn Timer     | 20 Seconds |
| Spectators     | Disabled   |
| Reconnection   | Enabled    |
| Match Duration | Standard   |

Ranked matches should provide a consistent competitive environment.

---

# 6. Ranking Philosophy

A rank should represent a player's long-term skill level, not just recent wins.

Progress should be steady rather than volatile, rewarding consistent performance over isolated victories.

---

# 7. Rank Structure

The ranking ladder consists of the following divisions.

| Rank         | Tiers       |
| ------------ | ----------- |
| Bronze       | III, II, I  |
| Silver       | III, II, I  |
| Gold         | III, II, I  |
| Platinum     | III, II, I  |
| Diamond      | III, II, I  |
| Master       | Single Tier |
| Grand Master | Single Tier |
| Legend       | Top Players |

The tier system provides frequent progression milestones while maintaining meaningful advancement.

---

# 8. Matchmaking Principles

The matchmaking system should prioritize:

1. Similar skill level.
2. Similar latency.
3. Similar recent performance.
4. Similar competitive division.

Wait times may increase slightly to preserve match quality.

---

# 9. Placement Matches

New competitive players begin with placement matches.

Recommended format:

* 10 placement games.

The purpose is to estimate initial skill level before assigning a permanent rank.

Placement should consider:

* Wins
* Opponent strength
* Individual performance
* Match completion

---

# 10. Promotion & Demotion

Players earn Rank Points (RP).

Promotion occurs when the required threshold is reached.

Demotion occurs when RP falls below the minimum for the current tier.

Movement should feel predictable rather than arbitrary.

---

# 11. Rank Points (RP)

Rank Points represent competitive progression.

Players gain RP for:

* Winning matches.
* Defeating higher-ranked opponents.
* Consistent performance.

Players lose RP for:

* Losing matches.
* Repeated inactivity.
* Competitive forfeits.

RP should not be influenced by cosmetic purchases or account age.

---

# 12. Season Structure

Competitive play is organized into seasons.

Recommended duration:

**Three months per season**

Each season introduces:

* Fresh leaderboard.
* Seasonal rewards.
* Cosmetic content.
* New competitive goals.

---

# 13. Seasonal Reset

At the start of each season:

* High-ranked players are partially reset.
* Lower-ranked players remain close to their previous rank.
* Cosmetic rewards are distributed.

Soft resets maintain competitive freshness while respecting previous achievements.

---

# 14. Seasonal Rewards

Season rewards recognize participation and achievement.

Examples:

* Exclusive titles.
* Animated profile frames.
* Ranked card backs.
* Seasonal avatars.
* Profile badges.

Rewards should be cosmetic only and never provide gameplay advantages.

---

# 15. Leaderboards

Leaderboards showcase top-performing players.

Categories may include:

* Global Ranking
* Regional Ranking
* Friends Ranking
* Seasonal Ranking
* Weekly Winners

Leaderboards should celebrate excellence while encouraging healthy competition.

---

# 16. Win Streaks

Winning consecutive ranked matches may grant small bonus RP within defined limits.

Win streaks should:

* Reward consistent performance.
* Avoid creating runaway progression.

Losses reset the streak.

---

# 17. Loss Protection

To reduce frustration:

Newly promoted players may receive temporary loss protection.

This allows players to adjust to stronger competition without immediate demotion.

---

# 18. Match Quality

A high-quality competitive match is defined by:

* Balanced skill levels.
* Stable connection.
* Full participation.
* Minimal waiting.
* Fair outcomes.

Quality should always take precedence over speed.

---

# 19. Competitive Integrity

Competitive integrity depends on:

* Official rules only.
* No hidden advantages.
* Consistent matchmaking.
* Reliable moderation.
* Transparent ranking.

Players must trust that every match is fair.

---

# 20. AFK Policy

Players who remain inactive during ranked matches negatively affect everyone.

Recommended policy:

First offense:

* Warning.

Repeated offenses:

* Temporary matchmaking restriction.

Persistent offenses:

* Increased penalties.

The system should distinguish between intentional inactivity and genuine connection issues where possible.

---

# 21. Disconnect Policy

Temporary disconnect:

Player receives a limited reconnection window.

If reconnection succeeds:

Match continues.

If reconnection fails:

Player forfeits.

Disconnect policies should balance fairness with technical realities.

---

# 22. Match Abandonment

Leaving a ranked match early counts as a loss.

Repeated abandonment may result in:

* Reduced RP.
* Temporary ranked suspension.
* Progressive cooldowns.

Players should be encouraged to complete every match.

---

# 23. Fair Play Expectations

Competitive players are expected to:

* Finish matches.
* Respect opponents.
* Avoid abusive communication.
* Report issues honestly.
* Follow official rules.

Sportsmanship contributes to a healthier competitive environment.

---

# 24. Anti-Smurf Philosophy

Smurfing undermines matchmaking quality.

Preventive measures may include:

* Ranked eligibility requirements.
* Placement matches.
* Rapid rank adjustment for highly skilled new accounts.
* Behavioral monitoring.

The goal is to place skilled players in appropriate divisions quickly.

---

# 25. Anti-Collusion

Players must not cooperate to manipulate rankings.

Examples include:

* Intentionally losing.
* Coordinated boosting.
* Shared accounts.
* Match fixing.

Violations may result in competitive penalties or account action.

---

# 26. Competitive Code of Conduct

Players should:

* Compete honestly.
* Respect opponents.
* Avoid harassment.
* Accept victory gracefully.
* Accept defeat respectfully.

Healthy competition strengthens the community.

---

# 27. Tournament Philosophy

Tournaments represent the highest level of competition.

Principles:

* Official rules.
* Equal conditions.
* Transparent administration.
* Clear scheduling.
* Public brackets.

Tournament participation should feel prestigious.

---

# 28. Tournament Formats

Supported formats may include:

* Single Elimination.
* Double Elimination.
* Round Robin.
* Swiss System.

Each format serves different event sizes and objectives.

---

# 29. Competitive Statistics

Players should have access to detailed competitive statistics.

Examples:

* Current Rank.
* Highest Rank.
* Seasonal Rank.
* Win Rate.
* Average Match Duration.
* Longest Win Streak.
* Favorite Card Color.
* Average Cards Remaining at Victory.

Statistics encourage self-improvement.

---

# 30. Ranked Profile

A competitive profile should display:

* Rank.
* Tier.
* Seasonal Badge.
* Peak Rank.
* Win Percentage.
* Total Ranked Matches.
* Achievements.
* Favorite Mode.

Profiles provide recognition without revealing unnecessary private information.

---

# 31. Competitive Rewards Philosophy

Competitive rewards should emphasize prestige rather than power.

Examples:

* Exclusive titles.
* Profile decorations.
* Animated borders.
* Seasonal card backs.
* Victory effects.

Gameplay balance should never depend on ranked rewards.

---

# 32. Competitive Accessibility

Ranked mode should remain welcoming.

The system should avoid:

* Excessive punishment.
* Hidden rules.
* Confusing progression.
* Unclear penalties.

Players should understand how to improve.

---

# 33. Competitive Health Metrics

Success can be evaluated using:

### Participation

* Ranked player count.
* Placement completion rate.
* Match completion rate.

### Match Quality

* Balanced matches.
* Average queue time.
* Disconnect rate.
* AFK rate.

### Community Health

* Reports per match.
* Sportsmanship indicators.
* Player retention.

These metrics help maintain a fair competitive ecosystem.

---

# 34. Competitive Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

* Pay-to-win progression.
* Hidden matchmaking adjustments.
* Excessive rank inflation.
* Unpredictable RP gains.
* Punishing experimentation.
* Overly long seasons.
* Frequent hard resets.

Competitive systems should build trust rather than confusion.

---

# 35. Design Checklist

Before introducing any competitive feature, verify:

* Does it improve fairness?
* Does it reward skill?
* Is progression transparent?
* Does it protect competitive integrity?
* Does it discourage abuse?
* Does it respect player time?
* Does it align with official rules?

If all answers are "yes," the feature supports the competitive vision.

---

# 36. Summary

This Competitive Design & Ranking System establishes the framework for a fair, transparent, and engaging ranked experience. It balances progression, recognition, and integrity while respecting both casual players aspiring to improve and dedicated competitors seeking mastery.

By emphasizing skill-based matchmaking, meaningful seasonal progression, cosmetic rewards, and clear behavioral expectations, the competitive ecosystem supports long-term engagement without compromising the core philosophy of fairness and accessibility established throughout the previous volumes.
